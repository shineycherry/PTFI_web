Introduction to Homomorphic Encryption (HE)

Homomorphic Encryption (HE) is a form of encryption that allows computations to be performed on encrypted data without the need to decrypt it first. This means sensitive data can be processed while remaining encrypted, ensuring privacy and security throughout the computation process. This feature makes HE particularly valuable in financial services, healthcare, and other industries where data privacy is critical.

How Homomorphic Encryption Works

  1. Data Encryption: Data is first encrypted using a public key, converting it into an encrypted format that is unreadable to anyone without the corresponding private key.
  2. Performing Operations on Encrypted Data: Once the data is encrypted, computations can be performed on this data without decrypting it. This means sensitive data can remain private even during processing.
  3. Decryption of Results: After the computations are complete, the encrypted result can be sent back to the data owner. The data owner uses their private key to decrypt the result, revealing the final computed outcome.

Benefits of Homomorphic Encryption in Financial Services

  • Data Privacy: Since data remains encrypted during computations, sensitive information is never exposed, ensuring high levels of privacy.
  • Secure Data Sharing: Organizations can share data for processing without risking unauthorized access, as the data remains encrypted.
  • Regulatory Compliance: HE helps meet strict privacy regulations, such as GDPR, by ensuring that personal data is always protected.
  • Fraud Detection and Risk Management: Financial institutions can analyze encrypted data to detect fraud or assess risks without exposing sensitive customer information.
